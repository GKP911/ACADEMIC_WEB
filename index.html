About

I specialize in the development of advanced tabletop ion sources and the study of beam dynamics for high-current electron and ion accelerators. My research extends to superconducting RF accelerators, encompassing engineering design, beam dynamics, higher-order mode analysis, and the investigation of beam instabilities.

Our group has indigenously developed a 50 keV tabletop Penning ion source capable of producing stable beams of H⁺, He⁺, N⁺, and Ar⁺ ions, with beam currents in the microampere range. This state-of-the-art source operates in the Compact Ion Source Research (cISR) Lab at the Physics Department, East Block. It employs plasma confinement under crossed electric and magnetic fields, enabling efficient ionization and beam extraction.

The generated ion beams are used for precise irradiation of thin-film systems, enabling nanoscale defect engineering. This allows systematic control over the structural, electronic, and optical properties of materials, paving the way for novel functionalities in advanced devices and cutting-edge applications.
