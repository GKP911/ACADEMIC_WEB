Research Areas

Our group works in the area of charged particle accelerators and beam physics, with a focus on the modeling and optimization of high-current electron guns and ion sources operating in both DC and radiofrequency (RF) regimes. We develop end-to-end designs of tabletop ion sources, including beam extraction, low-energy beam transport (LEBT), and associated diagnostic systems.

Our research focuses on ion beam–based defect engineering in thin film materials for controlled modification of their structural and functional properties.

In addition, our group investigates the modeling of superconducting radiofrequency (SRF) accelerating cavities, along with studies on beam dynamics, instability mechanisms, and mitigation of collective effects in high-intensity and high-brightness beams.
